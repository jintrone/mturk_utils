Read the following post and indicate the approximate percentage of words that are dedicated to telling stories.

For our purposes, a STORY is defined as as one or more episodes, where an episode consists of:

  • An initiating event—either internal (emotional or cognitive, such as “I became unhappy with my weight”) or external (e.g., “I developed a tumor”)
  • One or more intentional actions or a specific plan to engage in such actions to address the initiating event (e.g., "I went on a diet" or "I went for radiation treatments" or "I'm going for radiation treatments tomorrow"). Actions do not have to be written in first person. For example, "the doctor gave me a prescription for zoloft"; describes an intentional action taken by "the doctor."
  • Optionally, a set of consequences (e.g., "I didn't lose weight" or "The tumor shrank").

A story may contain contextual information that is needed to understand the story (e.g., "I have been overweight all my life" or "My father died of cancer"). Initiating events and intentional actions must be explicit (not implied) for text to be considered an episode.
